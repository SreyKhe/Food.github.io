Num Banhchok (Cambodai)

Num banhchok, kari sach moan, sour beef curry and curry leaf chicken. The word kari (ការី, kari) is used to designate an Indian type of curry and is believed to be a loanword from Tamil

Peking Duck (China)

Peking Duck is a revered icon in Chinese cuisine and is featured prominently on the Asian food list. This dish’s origins trace back to Imperial China and carries a rich history and cultural significance.

Kimchi (Korean Sauerkraut)

Kimchi, a staple in different types of Asian food, holds a top place in Korean meals and culture. Beyond being a side dish, kimchi enhances flavors and textures in Korean cuisine.

Laksa (Malaysian)

Let’s uncover the aromatic and spicy delight of Laksa. This Malaysian and Peranakan culinary gem has secured its place among the most popular foods in Asia, especially in the domain of the halal food market.

Biryani (Indian Rice-based Dish)

Biryani, a cherished dish among Asian food types, boasts diverse regional variations across India and neighboring countries, each presenting a unique culinary experience.

Pho (Vietnam)

Pho, one of the most popular foods in Asia, originates from Vietnam and carries deep cultural significance.

Nasi Goreng (Malaysia/Indonesia)

Nasi Goreng, a beloved dish in both Malaysia and Indonesia, reflects a shared love for its delectable taste. This fried rice dish, featured prominently on the Asian food list, captures the essence of Southeast Asian cuisine.

Tom Yam (Thailand)

Let’s talk about the bold and spicy vibe of Tom Yam, renowned as one of the best Asian foods with origins in Thailand. This vibrant soup is a sensory delight, boasting a harmonious blend of crucial ingredients like lemongrass, lime leaves, and chili.

Tonkatsu (Korea)

Tonkatsu, originating from Korea, is a straightforward yet flavorful dish. Its preparation involves a crispy breaded cutlet, typically pork, deep-fried to golden perfection. The appeal of Tonkatsu in Korean cuisine lies in its simplicity and satisfying taste.

Hainanese Chicken Rice (Singapore)

Step into the culinary world of Singapore with Hainanese Chicken Rice, a national treasure and one of the most popular foods in Asia.
